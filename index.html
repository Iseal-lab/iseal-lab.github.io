Laboratory

Next-Generation Intelligent Semiconductor Energy Engineering and Analysis Laboratory

i-SEAL is an international and interdisciplinary laboratory at Kyungpook National University. The laboratory conducts research on semiconductor materials, spectroscopy and characterization, surface and interface engineering, bandgap engineering, minority carrier control, and the prototyping of novel devices.

At a glance

  • International and interdisciplinary research team
  • Semiconductor materials, spectroscopy, and characterization
  • Surface/interface passivation, bandgap engineering, and minority carrier control
  • Power devices, solar cells, gas sensors, MEMS, and photodetectors

About

About i-SEAL

i-SEAL works across semiconductor materials, interfaces, and device applications. The laboratory pursues interdisciplinary research and training in semiconductor-energy-related fields.

Team

International and interdisciplinary group

The laboratory brings together researchers and students from interdisciplinary backgrounds including electronics and electrical engineering, computing, physics, chemistry, and mathematics.

Recruitment

Open to multiple levels

The laboratory recruits full-time research staff and engineers, postdoctoral researchers, postgraduate students, and undergraduate students.

Vision

Semiconductor energy initiatives

The laboratory develops and promotes semiconductor energy initiatives through materials research, interface engineering, and novel device prototyping.
