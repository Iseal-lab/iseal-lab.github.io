Laboratory

Next-Generation Intelligent Semiconductor Energy Engineering and Analysis Laboratory

The i-SEAL Laboratory at Kyungpook National University conducts research on semiconductor materials, surface and interface engineering, carrier control, and device-oriented applications. The laboratory is interested in both fundamental understanding and practical device development, with research spanning materials fabrication, characterization, and the prototyping of semiconductor and energy-related systems.

About

About i-SEAL

i-SEAL is an interdisciplinary research group working at the intersection of semiconductor science, device engineering, and energy-related technologies. The laboratory focuses on building a coherent pathway from materials design and analysis to device-level implementation while supporting graduate training and collaborative academic research.

Focus

Materials to devices

The laboratory connects materials processing, physical analysis, and device-oriented design in order to study semiconductor systems across multiple scales.

Research

Academic and practical relevance

Research activities address both fundamental scientific questions and application-driven device concepts in semiconductor and energy-related fields.

Environment

Graduate training and collaboration

i-SEAL provides an academic environment for graduate research, interdisciplinary discussion, and collaboration with researchers working in related areas.

Research Highlights

Core research directions

  • Semiconductor materials fabrication, spectroscopy, and characterization
  • Surface and interface engineering
  • Bandgap engineering and minority carrier control
  • Novel device prototyping
