Welcome

Next-Generation Intelligent Semiconductor Energy Engineering and Analysis Laboratory

The Next-Generation Intelligent Semiconductor Energy Engineering and Analysis Laboratory (i-SEAL) welcomes you. We are an international and interdisciplinary team of scientists, engineers, researchers, and students at Kyungpook National University.

Our research spans semiconductor materials fabrication, spectroscopy and characterization, surface and interface passivation, bandgap engineering, minority carrier control, and the prototyping of novel devices including power devices, solar cells, gas sensors, microelectromechanical devices, and photodetectors.

Introduction

About the laboratory

i-SEAL brings together researchers from interdisciplinary backgrounds including electronics and electrical engineering, computing, physics, chemistry, and mathematics. The laboratory develops and promotes semiconductor energy initiatives through materials research, interface engineering, device analysis, and novel device prototyping.

Team

International and interdisciplinary group

The laboratory includes researchers and students from multiple backgrounds and supports collaborative work across theory, experiment, characterization, and device-oriented research.

Recruitment

Open to multiple levels

We welcome full-time research staff and engineers, postdoctoral researchers, postgraduate students, and undergraduate students.

Vision

Semiconductor energy initiatives

The laboratory aims to advance semiconductor-energy-related research through materials development, interface engineering, and practical device prototyping.
