Laboratory

Next-Generation Intelligent Semiconductor Energy Engineering and Analysis Laboratory

The i-SEAL Laboratory at Kyungpook National University conducts research on semiconductor materials, surface and interface engineering, carrier control, and device-oriented applications. The laboratory is interested in both fundamental understanding and practical device development, with research spanning materials fabrication, characterization, and the prototyping of semiconductor and energy-related systems.

Laboratory Overview

  • School of Electronics Engineering, Kyungpook National University
  • Principal Investigator: Prof. Ju, Gwanghwi
  • Research spanning materials, interfaces, carrier behavior, and device implementation
  • Graduate-level academic research and collaboration environment

About

About i-SEAL

i-SEAL is an interdisciplinary research group working at the intersection of semiconductor science, device engineering, and energy-related technologies. The laboratory focuses on connecting materials-level understanding with device-level analysis and implementation through sustained academic research.

Focus

Materials to devices

The laboratory studies semiconductor systems from materials processing and characterization to interface control and device-oriented design.

Approach

Analysis and implementation

Research combines physical understanding, engineering analysis, and practical prototyping for semiconductor and energy-related applications.

Environment

Graduate research community

i-SEAL supports graduate training, interdisciplinary discussion, and academic collaboration in closely related research areas.

Research Highlights

Core research directions

  • Semiconductor materials fabrication, spectroscopy, and characterization
  • Surface and interface engineering
  • Bandgap engineering and minority carrier control
  • Novel device prototyping
