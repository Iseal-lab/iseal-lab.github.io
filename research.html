Research

Research

Core research areas of the Next-Generation Intelligent Semiconductor Energy Engineering and Analysis Laboratory.

Research Areas

Main research directions

Semiconductor Materials Fabrication, Spectroscopy, and Characterization

Research on manufacturing techniques, spectroscopy, and characterization methods for semiconductor materials and related functional systems.

Surface and Interface Passivation and Coating

Surface and interface engineering strategies for improved material quality, device stability, and enhanced semiconductor performance.

Bandgap Engineering and Minority Carrier Control

Device-oriented studies on band structure design, minority carrier behavior, and semiconductor-energy-related applications.

Novel Device Prototyping

Prototyping and analysis of advanced devices including power devices, solar cells, gas sensors, microelectromechanical devices, and photodetectors.

Approach

Interdisciplinary research and training

The laboratory connects materials, interfaces, processing, characterization, and device concepts. We emphasize interdisciplinary collaboration across electronics and electrical engineering, physics, chemistry, computing, and related fields.
